Abstract

Though Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) hold immense potential for transforming the Low-Altitude Economy, developing an agent that combines both intelligence and flexibility remains a significant challenge. To this end, we propose a novel aerial navigation system, AeroSeek, to search executable paths that coherent transition from cognitive understanding to physical movement, enabling more autonomous, flexible navigation with instruction guidance. Specifically, given a navigation instruction, we follow a fast-slow thinking procedure of the drone pilot by breaking down complex decisions into manageable sub-tasks and initiating motion planning immediately upon completion of each sub-task for real-time movement. By integrating multi-Chain-of-Thought, AeroSeek is capable of reasoning along with spatial recognition and self-verification to search for robust sub-goals in terms of expected constraints. For sub-goal navigation, we introduce OnKi-RRT, the brand-new motion planner approach featuring both kinodynamic constraints and asymptotic optimality, while ensuring dynamic obstacle avoidance for UAVs. Benefiting from the proposed two-tier searching protocol, AeroSeek performs advanced comprehensive capabilities of complicated instruction and adapts to dynamic aerial conditions. Extended experiments demonstrate the advantages of AeroSeek across tasks of varying complexity.
